Maxim Bontemps-Gaborit is an agronomy and agri-food engineer from Institut Agro
Montpellier, currently engaged in a double-degree program with a second-year MSc
in Biotechnology, Microbiology, and Innovation for Health at the University of
Montpellier. Throughout his academic training, he has developed a strong
interest in fermentation and food-related microbial innovation, with a
particular focus on microbiota and the One Health approach.

This interest led him to carry out his final-year internship in the Food
Microbial Ecology Laboratory at the Micalis Institute. Under the supervision of
[Julien Tap](/team/julien-tap) and [William Bourelle](/team/william-bourelle), 
he is involved in the [MicroEngine](project/microengine/) research
project. His internship focuses on refining and validating microbial consortia
identified through in silico modeling and experimental screening, by leveraging
metatranscriptomics to unravel metabolic interactions and strain helper effects.
This integrative step is crucial for identifying functional synergies between
strains and for improving model predictions of strain compatibility and
robustness.
